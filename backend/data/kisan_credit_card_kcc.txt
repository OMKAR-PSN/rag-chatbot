# SCHEME NAME: Kisan Credit Card (KCC)
# ALSO KNOWN AS: KCC, Kisan Card, farmer credit card, agricultural loan card, kisan karj card
# KEYWORDS: kisan credit card, KCC loan, farmer loan, 4 percent interest loan, khet ke liye loan, bina guarantee loan, agricultural credit
# CATEGORY: Farmer, Finance
# LAUNCHED BY: Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, NABARD
# OFFICIAL WEBSITE: pmkisan.gov.in, nabard.org
# HELPLINE: 1800-180-1551 (NABARD), Bank branch helplines

---

## WHAT IS THIS SCHEME:
Kisan Credit Card gives farmers an easy short-term loan of up to Rs 3 lakh (three lakh rupees) to buy seeds, fertilisers, pesticides, and other farming needs. The interest rate is only 4 percent per year which is much lower than any private moneylender. The farmer can use the money as needed and repay after harvest. Farmers do not need to give any property as guarantee for loans up to Rs 1.6 lakh.

---

## WHO CAN APPLY (ELIGIBILITY):
- All farmers who own or cultivate land including tenant farmers and sharecroppers
- Farmers involved in allied activities like animal husbandry and fisheries are also eligible
- Self Help Groups (SHG) and Joint Liability Groups (JLG) of farmers can also apply
- No upper age limit mentioned but banks may have their own criteria
- Must have land records or lease agreement
- Must have a bank account

WHO CANNOT APPLY:
- Non-farmers (persons not involved in agriculture or allied activities)

---

## WHAT WILL I GET (BENEFITS):
- Revolving credit limit up to Rs 3 lakh (three lakh rupees) for crop loans
- Interest rate of only 4 percent per year (after 3% government interest subvention)
- No collateral (guarantee) required for loans up to Rs 1.6 lakh
- Flexible repayment — repay after harvest, not in fixed monthly instalments
- Rs 50,000 (fifty thousand rupees) for personal and household needs as part of KCC limit
- Free personal accident insurance of Rs 50,000 for death and Rs 25,000 for disability
- ATM card facility — withdraw cash as needed throughout the season

---

## WHAT DOCUMENTS DO I NEED:
- Aadhaar card
- Land ownership documents or lease agreement or Khatian
- Passport size photographs (2 copies)
- Bank account passbook
- Application form from bank
- For loans above Rs 1.6 lakh — land documents for mortgage

---

## HOW TO APPLY (STEP BY STEP):
1. Visit your nearest bank branch — government banks like SBI, Bank of India, PNB, or cooperative banks
2. Ask for Kisan Credit Card application form
3. Fill the form with your land details and farming requirements
4. Submit with Aadhaar, land documents, and photographs
5. Bank officer may visit your farm for verification
6. KCC is issued within 2 weeks of application
7. A credit limit is set based on your land size and crop type
8. Use the card to withdraw cash or make purchases at agricultural shops
9. Repay the loan after harvest — the credit limit gets restored for the next season
10. Call your bank helpline or NABARD at 1800-180-1551 for help

---

## COMMON QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS:

Q: I am a tenant farmer. Do I own land? Can I still apply for KCC?
A: Yes. Tenant farmers and sharecroppers can apply for Kisan Credit Card. You will need a lease agreement or a letter from the landowner. You do not need to own the land yourself.

Q: What is the interest rate on KCC loan?
A: The interest rate on KCC is 4 percent per year if you repay on time. The actual bank rate is 7 percent but the government gives 3 percent subsidy. If you do not repay on time, the rate goes back to 7 percent or higher.

Q: How much loan can I get on KCC?
A: The loan amount depends on your land size and the crop you grow. Typically Rs 10,000 to Rs 15,000 per acre is the base amount. The maximum for crop loan is Rs 3 lakh at 4 percent interest.

Q: My KCC limit is Rs 1 lakh but I need Rs 2 lakh. What do I do?
A: Visit your bank and ask for a KCC limit enhancement. Carry your land documents and show the bank your farming needs and repayment history. Banks can increase the limit.

Q: I repaid my KCC loan but the bank is charging extra interest. What should I do?
A: Visit the bank branch with your payment receipts. If the issue is not resolved, complain to the Banking Ombudsman or call 14448 (RBI helpline).

Q: My crop failed this year due to drought. Do I still have to repay KCC?
A: If you have PM Fasal Bima insurance, file a claim first. For KCC repayment, visit your bank and explain the crop failure. Banks are allowed to give repayment extensions or restructure the loan in such cases.

Q: Can I use KCC money for household expenses or only for farming?
A: KCC includes a sub-limit of Rs 50,000 for personal and household expenses. But the main KCC amount should be used for farming inputs like seeds, fertilisers, and pesticides.

Q: My KCC expired. Can I renew it?
A: Yes. Visit your bank before or after expiry with your KCC card and documents. Renewal is simple if you have repaid previous loans on time. A good repayment history helps get higher limits.

---

## IMPORTANT WARNINGS:
- Repay KCC loans on time to maintain the 4 percent interest rate — late payment increases interest
- Never give your KCC PIN or card to anyone including relatives
- No government officer charges a fee to process KCC — report anyone asking for money
- Do not take KCC from private moneylenders — only from registered banks

---

## PLAIN SUMMARY:
Kisan Credit Card gives farmers easy loans up to Rs 3 lakh at only 4 percent interest per year for buying seeds, fertilisers, and farming needs. Both land-owning and tenant farmers can apply at any government bank branch with their land documents and Aadhaar. Repayment is flexible — pay after the harvest. Helpline: 1800-180-1551.
