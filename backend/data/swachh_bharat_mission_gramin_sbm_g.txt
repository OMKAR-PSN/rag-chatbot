# SCHEME NAME: Swachh Bharat Mission Gramin (SBM-G)
# ALSO KNOWN AS: Swachh Bharat, toilet scheme, ODF scheme, shauchalay yojana, free toilet scheme, nirmal bharat
# KEYWORDS: free toilet, shauchalay, ODF village, toilet money, Rs 12000 toilet, open defecation free, gramin toilet, BPL toilet
# CATEGORY: Housing, Sanitation
# LAUNCHED BY: Ministry of Jal Shakti
# OFFICIAL WEBSITE: sbm.gov.in/sbmgNew
# HELPLINE: 1800-11-4999

---

## WHAT IS THIS SCHEME:
Swachh Bharat Mission Gramin gives Rs 12,000 (twelve thousand rupees) to every rural household that does not have a toilet to build a toilet in their home. The goal is to make every village free from open defecation. The money is sent in two instalments directly to the bank account of the household member after the toilet is built and verified.

---

## WHO CAN APPLY (ELIGIBILITY):
- Any rural household that does not currently have a toilet at home
- Households listed in the SECC 2011 data as not having toilet facility
- Both BPL and APL households in rural areas can apply
- Special priority for households with women, elderly persons, or disabled persons
- SC and ST households are given priority

WHO CANNOT APPLY:
- Rural households that already have a functioning toilet
- Urban households — they apply under SBM Urban with different procedures

---

## WHAT WILL I GET (BENEFITS):
- Rs 12,000 (twelve thousand rupees) total for construction of one toilet
- Money is paid in two instalments — first instalment before construction, second after completion
- Some states add additional amount on top of Rs 12,000 from their own budget
- Free technical support and design from Gram Panchayat
- Community toilets are built free of cost in public areas

---

## WHAT DOCUMENTS DO I NEED:
- Aadhaar card
- Bank account passbook linked to Aadhaar
- SECC data registration or BPL card (in most states)
- Photograph of the house showing no existing toilet

---

## HOW TO APPLY (STEP BY STEP):
1. Visit your Gram Panchayat office and inform them that your house does not have a toilet
2. Ask to be registered under Swachh Bharat Mission
3. Your name will be verified against SECC data or a local survey
4. First instalment is transferred to your bank account
5. You construct the toilet using the money — Gram Panchayat provides the design
6. A local official or Swachh Bharat Prerak verifies the toilet construction
7. Second instalment is sent after verification
8. Your house is marked as ODF (Open Defecation Free)
9. Call 1800-11-4999 for complaints or queries

---

## COMMON QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS:

Q: I already built a toilet two years ago using my own money. Can I still get the Rs 12,000?
A: No. The Rs 12,000 is only for households that are building a toilet fresh. If you already built a toilet, you are not eligible for this assistance.

Q: My village sarpanch says my name is not in the list. What do I do?
A: Visit the Block Development Office and ask for your name to be added to the beneficiary list. You can also check your name at sbm.gov.in. Carry your Aadhaar and SECC registration proof.

Q: I got Rs 12,000 but it is not enough to build a toilet. What can I do?
A: You can use MGNREGA wages to help with construction labour. The Rs 12,000 is for basic toilet construction — you can add your own money if you want a better toilet.

Q: When will the money come to my bank account?
A: First instalment comes after registration and approval by Gram Panchayat. Second instalment comes after the toilet is built and inspected. Total time is usually 1 to 3 months.

Q: Who verifies that the toilet is built?
A: The Gram Panchayat Secretary or a Swachh Bharat Prerak (local motivator) visits your home and verifies the toilet construction before the second instalment is released.

Q: I am a tenant, not the landowner. Can I still get toilet money?
A: This varies by state. Generally, the assistance is given to the household not the landowner. Check with your Gram Panchayat about rules in your state.

---

## IMPORTANT WARNINGS:
- Do not pay anyone to register you under this scheme — registration is free
- Gram Panchayat officer cannot demand a commission for releasing your money
- Build the toilet according to the recommended design — single pit or twin pit toilets
- Report corruption at 1800-11-4999

---

## PLAIN SUMMARY:
Swachh Bharat Mission gives Rs 12,000 to rural households to build a toilet at home. Any rural family without a toilet can apply at the Gram Panchayat office. The money comes directly to the bank account in two instalments. Helpline: 1800-11-4999.
