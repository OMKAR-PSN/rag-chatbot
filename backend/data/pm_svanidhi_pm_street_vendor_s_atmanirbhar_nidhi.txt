# SCHEME NAME: PM SVANidhi (PM Street Vendor's AtmaNirbhar Nidhi)
# ALSO KNOWN AS: SVANidhi, Street Vendor loan, rehdi wala loan, hawker loan, Rs 10000 vendor loan, svanidhi yojana
# KEYWORDS: street vendor loan, rehdi patri loan, hawker loan, Rs 10000 working capital, vendor business loan, svanidhi, footpath seller loan
# CATEGORY: Employment, Livelihood
# LAUNCHED BY: Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs
# OFFICIAL WEBSITE: pmsvanidhi.mohua.gov.in
# HELPLINE: 1800-11-1979

---

## WHAT IS THIS SCHEME:
PM SVANidhi gives small working capital loans to street vendors — people who sell vegetables, fruits, food, clothes, or any goods on the roadside or footpath. The first loan is Rs 10,000 (ten thousand rupees) with no collateral required. If repaid on time, the vendor gets a bigger loan of Rs 20,000 and then Rs 50,000. The scheme also gives a digital payment incentive of Rs 1,200 per year to vendors who accept UPI or QR code payments.

---

## WHO CAN APPLY (ELIGIBILITY):
- Street vendors who were vending on or before March 24, 2020
- Vendors with a vending certificate or identity card issued by Urban Local Body
- Vendors who have been surveyed during town vending committee surveys
- Vendors who have received a recommendation letter from Urban Local Body or town vending committee
- Both urban and peri-urban vendors are eligible

---

## WHAT WILL I GET (BENEFITS):
- First loan: Rs 10,000 (ten thousand rupees) — no collateral needed
- Second loan: Rs 20,000 after timely repayment of first loan
- Third loan: Rs 50,000 after timely repayment of second loan
- Digital payment incentive: Rs 100 per month (Rs 1,200 per year) for accepting UPI payments
- Credit score building — good repayment helps you get bigger loans from banks in future
- Interest subsidy of 7 percent — government pays part of the interest

---

## WHAT DOCUMENTS DO I NEED:
- Aadhaar card
- Vendor certificate or Identity card from Urban Local Body (municipality)
- If no certificate — recommendation letter from town vending committee or Urban Local Body
- Bank account or mobile payment details
- Mobile number linked to Aadhaar

---

## HOW TO APPLY (STEP BY STEP):
1. Visit pmsvanidhi.mohua.gov.in on mobile or computer
2. Click on Apply Loan and select your loan type (first, second, or third)
3. Enter your Aadhaar number and mobile number for verification
4. Fill in your business details and vendor certificate number
5. Select a lending institution — Small Finance Bank, MFI, cooperative bank, or SHG
6. The lender will verify your details and disburse the loan within 30 days
7. Repay in 12 monthly instalments
8. You can also apply through CSC centre or local bank branch
9. Helpline: 1800-11-1979 (toll free)

---

## COMMON QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS:

Q: I sell vegetables on a handcart. Do I qualify for SVANidhi?
A: Yes. Vegetable vendors on handcarts are exactly the target group for PM SVANidhi. You need a vendor certificate or identity card from your city municipality. Visit the municipality office first to get your vendor card.

Q: I don't have a vendor certificate. Can I still apply?
A: Yes. If you do not have a vendor certificate, you can still apply if you get a recommendation letter from your local Urban Local Body (municipality) or the town vending committee. Visit the municipality office and explain your situation.

Q: I repaid my Rs 10,000 loan on time. When do I get the Rs 20,000 loan?
A: After you repay the first Rs 10,000 loan fully, you can immediately apply for the second loan of Rs 20,000 on the same SVANidhi website or through the same lender.

Q: What is the interest rate on SVANidhi loan?
A: The lender charges up to 24 percent interest per year. But the government gives a 7 percent subsidy, so your effective rate is around 17 percent. This is much less than a moneylender who charges 100 to 200 percent.

Q: How do I get the Rs 1,200 digital payment incentive?
A: Accept payments from customers via UPI, QR code, or any digital payment method. The system tracks your transactions and deposits Rs 100 per month directly to your bank account if you meet the monthly target.

Q: The municipality is not giving me a vendor certificate. What do I do?
A: File a complaint with the District Magistrate or call the SVANidhi helpline 1800-11-1979. Street vendors have a legal right to vend under the Street Vendors Act 2014.

Q: I took the loan but my business failed and I cannot repay. What happens?
A: Contact your lender and explain the situation. Do not ignore the repayment. Lenders can sometimes give a repayment extension or restructure the loan. Non-payment will affect your credit score.

---

## IMPORTANT WARNINGS:
- Never take SVANidhi loan from a private moneylender — only from registered banks and microfinance institutions listed on the portal
- No agent or municipality official should charge a fee to help you apply
- Keep all loan repayment receipts safely
- Report fraud at 1800-11-1979

---

## PLAIN SUMMARY:
PM SVANidhi gives street vendors loans starting from Rs 10,000 without any collateral. Vendors who sell on roadsides, handcarts, or footpaths can apply with their vendor card from the municipality. On-time repayment leads to bigger loans of Rs 20,000 and then Rs 50,000. Helpline: 1800-11-1979.
